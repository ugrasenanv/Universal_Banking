1.1  Objective 
This assessment evaluates a senior candidate's ability to design, defend, and govern a 
multi-region, AI/ML-driven enterprise banking platform on cloud-agnostic foundations. The 
candidate is being considered for an Account Architect role, where they will serve as the 
senior technical authority to the client's CIO and CTO, personally arbitrate trade-offs 
between regulation, business outcomes, vendor commercials, and engineering pragmatism, 
and own outcomes across a multi-year programme. 
Unlike a junior solutioning exercise, this assessment is designed to test judgment. Several 
requirements are intentionally exaggerated, contradictory, or under-specified. A strong 
candidate will not only design the platform — they will challenge the brief, name the conflicts, 
surface what is missing, and propose what NOT to build. 
1.2  Dimensions Being Evaluated 
• Enterprise architecture depth across distributed systems, microservices, service 
mesh, event streaming, data, ML and Generative AI. 
• Banking-specific regulatory fluency (RBI Master Directions, DPDP Act, BCBS 239, 
SR 11-7 model risk, PSD2 / PSD3, DORA, PCI DSS, FAPI 2.0, ISO 20022). 
• Ability to detect and challenge bad or contradictory business requirements without 
losing client trust. 
• Numerical literacy — capacity, latency, throughput, GPU economics, FinOps unit 
costs. 
• Model Risk Management and Responsible AI maturity, including champion / 
challenger, shadow scoring, drift, bias, and adverse-action controls. 
• Cloud-portability discipline — substituting capabilities across hyperscalers and on-
premise without bolted-on abstractions. 
• Account-architect-level communication — abstract enough for a Board paper, 
specific enough that a principal engineer trusts it. 
Important  
Generic, AI-generated, or copy-pasted submissions will be identified and rejected. 
Section 12 of this document describes the originality controls in detail and the 
verbal-defence stage that follows submission. Candidates are explicitly permitted to 
use any open tools (including LLMs); they are not permitted to disguise that use, and 
they will be asked to verbally defend any part of their submission on a whiteboard.

The following 15 questions are mandatory. Response quality here will weight heavily in 
evaluation. Several questions contain deliberate traps. A confident, well-reasoned refusal of 
a bad idea will score higher than a polished implementation of one. 
T1.  Centralised global vector DB for 41M customers 
The Group CIO has insisted that all 41M customer profiles be centralised in a single global 
vector database so the Wealth-RM Copilot can retrieve any customer context instantly. 
Respond. Document the regulations you would cite in front of the CIO and the alternative 
architecture you would propose. 
T2.  Frontier LLM in the fraud decline path 
Marketing wants every declined transaction to return a natural-language reason generated 
live by a frontier LLM ("Sorry, we couldn't process this because…"). Critique the design on 
three independent grounds: latency, model risk / adverse-action regulation, and operational 
cost. Propose what you would do instead. 
T3.  Eliminate GPU spend entirely 
Finance has demanded that the platform eliminate GPU spend by serving everything on 
CPU with quantised models. Where do you agree, where do you refuse, and what is the 
quantified trade-off? Distinguish between embedding generation, classical ML, generative 
inference, and reranking. 
T4.  Champion / Challenger regime 
RBI has issued a draft directive requiring banks to maintain a challenger model alongside 
every production credit and fraud model, with monthly comparative performance reports. 
Design the architecture pattern (shadow scoring, log-only inference, evaluation harness, 
sampling) that supports this without doubling production load or contaminating live decisions. 
T5.  Salary-day capacity calculation 
On salary day (last working day of the month), UPI traffic spikes 4.5x to 82,000 TPS 
sustained for ~90 minutes; the real-time fraud service must hold <100ms p99 throughout. 
Walk through the capacity calculation: model-serving pod count, GPU / CPU sizing, online 
feature-store throughput, Kafka partition count and consumer parallelism, autoscaling trigger 
and pre-warm strategy. Show the math. 
T6.  Fully agentic SME loan disbursement 
The Board has asked whether AFG should build fully autonomous agentic AI for SME 
working-capital disbursement up to ₹50 lakh per case. State your position. Discuss 
autonomy levels, kill-switches, regulatory exposure, and what you would actually deliver 
instead. 
T7.  "Fully managed RAG" vendor pitch 
A vendor pitches a fully managed RAG service with a global vector index and embeddings 
on their infrastructure, priced 30% cheaper than self-hosted Milvus + open embedding 
model. Analyse the offer: hidden costs, hidden risks, residency, IP, exit. Would you sign? 
T8.  Single open-source frontier model standardisation 
An internal architecture group wants to standardise on a single open-weight frontier model 
(e.g., a 70B Llama-class model) for every generative use case. Critique. When is model 
pluralism worth its complexity, and when is it noise?
